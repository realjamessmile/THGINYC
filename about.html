SYNOPSIS

This is a coming-of-age story about two girls realizing they have agency over the choices they make after years of letting life, boys, drugs, and Tess’s cult past bend them over a table. On April 14th, 1984, a series of unfortunate events send the girls spiraling toward rock bottom at an inextinguishable pace, forcing them to face the discomfort of living in reality, all the way down in the pits of being $5,000 in debt (approx. equivalent to $15,287.34 in 2025). A stroke of grace leads them to the office of Rabbi Bellamy, a wise Bostonian lesbian who came of age herself at the dawn of the United States Gay rights movement, who asks them to consider what on earth they’ve been doing with their lives, and whether or not they’d like to remain asleep at the wheel. No, say the girls. We’d like to fuck the world back. Thus marks the start of the girls’ quest for world domination, as they embark on a journey to learn how to harness the full potential of their power.

Information about the Company

Toonieroonie Productions is a comedic animation company based in Brooklyn, New York. Owners Tess Lancaster and Riley Kennedy Stark, who independently perform sketch comedy and improv, expanded the company this year to include stage performance. Toonieroonie’s first performance of The Horniest Girls in New York City was held on March 14th with The Village Salon in Manhattan. A link to the performance has been provided in the “Video Link” response section. This would be the company’s first time to Fringe (exciting!).

Preferred Space

Jersey

Why do you like this space?

Jersey is perfect for The Horniest Girls in New York City. Our main needs are all addressed by the space:quick access to a backstage area for costume and prop changes, a wide enough stage with wingspace on either side to support our “split stage” scenes, seating space for at least one wheelchair user, great sightlines, and right under 100 seats. The venue looks beautiful, and we know our show would fit right in. Also, we would most likely be flying to you from the New Jersey airport, so it would be great if we could tell everyone what the real Jersey is like when we return home.
